ORIGIN:
The word "security" originates from the Latin term "\(securus\)", meaning "free from care" or "safe," which is a combination of "\(se-\)" (without) and "\(cura\)" (care). This origin reflects the core idea of being free from danger, anxiety, or worry, a sense that evolved to include modern concepts like financial and national security.      

---

### Latin root:
"Security" comes from the Latin word "\(securus\)", which literally means "without care".  

---

### Components:
"\(Securus\)" is made up of two parts - 
"\(se-\)" meaning "without," and "\(cura\)" meaning "care".  

---

### Core meaning:
This etymology points to the fundamental meaning of security as a state of being safe, free from harm, danger, or anxiety.  

---

### Evolution of meaning:
From its origin, the term expanded to encompass financial security (assets pledged as a guarantee) and later, national security, particularly after the mid-20th century.
