# ORIGIN:
The word "networking" originates from the noun "network," which combines "net" and "work" to describe a mesh-like arrangement. The term "networking" itself first appeared in the 1940s, referring to the building of connections in systems, and was later adopted in the 1980s to describe the social practice of building professional relationships. 

---

### Early origins:
The word "network" emerged in the 1550s to describe a physical "net-like arrangement". This meaning expanded to include complex systems like transport and broadcasting in the 1800s and early 1900s. 

---

### First use of "networking":
The word "networking" was first recorded in the 1940s, referring to the process of creating or operating a network, according to the Oxford English Dictionary. 

---

### Computer networks:
By the 1960s, the term "network" was being used to describe interconnected computers, and "networking" came to describe the process of establishing these systems in the 1980s. 

---

### Social and professional networking:
The meaning of "networking" as the "exchange of information and ideas among people with a common profession or special interest" became popular in the late 1970s or early 1980s, coinciding with the growth of professional environments and the need for professional connections.
