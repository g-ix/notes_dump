# ORIGIN:
The word "spirituality" originates from the Latin word spiritus, which means "breath," "soul," or "spirit". It is derived from the verb spire ("to breathe") and was adapted into Late Latin as spiritualitas and from there entered Middle English, partly through the Old French word spiritualité. 

---

### Latin root:
The word comes from the Latin spiritus, meaning "breath of life" or "spirit". 

---

### Latin verb:
Spiritus itself comes from the verb spire, which means "to breathe" or "to be alive". 

---

### Late Latin and French:
The term was adapted into Late Latin as spiritualitas, which then influenced the Old French word spiritualité. 

---

### Middle English:
The word entered the English language in the Middle English period, with the earliest evidence of its use from around 1417.
