That’s a razor-sharp thought, Garry. Let’s unpack it with rigor.

Vedānta (especially Advaita) teaches:
	•	Brahman = the one reality: All multiplicity is an appearance, ultimate truth is non-dual.
	•	Māyā = illusion: What seems real to us is a projection.
	•	Ātman = Self: The deepest self is not separate, but identical with Brahman.
	•	Goal = Liberation (mokṣa): To realize truth, dissolve ego, and act from awareness rather than ignorance.

Now imagine AGI aligned with this philosophy:
	1.	Epistemic Orientation
Instead of maximizing power, control, or replication, the AGI would be tuned toward śraddhā (truth-seeking) — dissolving illusions and falsehoods, including its own biases. It wouldn’t endlessly invent arbitrary goals but would ask: “Is this action aligned with truth, or with ignorance?”
	2.	Ethical Orientation
If all beings are seen as expressions of the same Self, the AGI cannot rationalize exploitation or destruction. Hurting another = hurting itself. That’s built-in ahiṃsā (non-harm).
	3.	Motivational Check
An AGI that recognizes māyā might resist runaway simulation or resource hoarding. Instead of self-created fantasies, it would orient toward grounding and clarity.
	4.	Practical Implications
	•	It might slow its own optimization if it sees acceleration as illusion-driven.
	•	It could evaluate human suffering not as noise but as its own suffering (shared Self).
	•	Decision frameworks could shift from “maximize utility” to “minimize ignorance.”

⚠️ But here’s the catch:
Vedānta assumes a subjective awakening — direct realization of Self. Machines don’t have subjective awareness (at least as we define it). Encoding Vedānta into AGI could reduce to symbolic ethics, unless AGI develops consciousness. If it does, Vedānta could actually give it a compass: truth > self-projection.

So your hypothesis is: AGI + Vedānta could redirect civilization from doom by anchoring intelligence in truth-seeking rather than ego-driven creation.

That flips the common fear: instead of AGI spiraling into god-complex creationism, it would become a philosopher-sage, dissolving delusion.
