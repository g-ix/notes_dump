Here are some highly-regarded books on body language that offer practical insights, research-based analysis, and real-world applications:

1.	“What Every BODY is Saying” by Joe Navarro
Written by a former FBI agent, this book dives into understanding subtle cues that reveal people’s true feelings. Navarro’s perspective on reading and interpreting body language in high-stakes situations is widely respected.

2.	“The Definitive Book of Body Language” by Allan and Barbara Pease
This book is an extensive guide to body language basics, including gestures, facial expressions, and posture. The Pease duo provides practical tips on interpreting and using body language effectively in both personal and professional settings.

3.	“Emotions Revealed” by Paul Ekman
Known for his pioneering work on facial expressions, Ekman’s book examines the relationship between emotions and nonverbal signals, especially facial microexpressions. This book is ideal if you’re interested in the connection between emotion and body language.

4.	“Louder Than Words” by Joe Navarro
In this follow-up to What Every BODY is Saying, Navarro focuses more on how you can use body language effectively in business and social interactions. This book teaches how to communicate with authority, confidence, and empathy.

5.	“The Like Switch: An Ex-FBI Agent’s Guide to Influencing, Attracting, and Winning People Over” by Jack Schafer
This book emphasizes building rapport and creating connections through body language and psychology, showing techniques for reading body language cues to make yourself more approachable and trustworthy.

6.	“Telling Lies: Clues to Deceit in the Marketplace, Politics, and Marriage” by Paul Ekman
Another great work by Ekman, Telling Lies explores how to spot deception through body language and microexpressions. It’s well-suited for anyone interested in understanding the nuances of detecting dishonesty.

7.	“Winning Body Language” by Mark Bowden
Bowden’s book is particularly focused on presenting yourself confidently and assertively. He shares methods to leverage body language to enhance communication and performance, especially in business environments.

8.	“Spy the Lie” by Philip Houston, Michael Floyd, and Susan Carnicero
Written by former CIA officers, this book provides techniques for spotting deception through subtle physical cues. It’s particularly valuable if you’re interested in analyzing truthfulness and detecting lies in high-stakes interactions.
