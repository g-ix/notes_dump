# Quantum Chemistry
Quantum chemistry applies the principles of quantum mechanics to understand the behavior of electrons in atoms and molecules, which in turn helps explain chemical bonding, reactions, and molecular structures. Quantum chemistry uses the following key concepts:

---

## 1. Atomic Orbitals and Electron Configuration:
Electrons exist in regions around the nucleus called "orbitals," each with specific energy levels. Quantum chemistry explains how these orbitals are structured (s, p, d, f orbitals) and how electrons are arranged in these orbitals based on quantum numbers (n, l, m, and spin).

---

## 2. Pauli Exclusion Principle:
No two electrons can have the same set of quantum numbers in an atom. This explains why electrons fill different orbitals and leads to the periodic table structure and the properties of elements.

---

## 3. Chemical Bonding:
- Quantum chemistry explains covalent and ionic bonds in terms of the wavefunctions of electrons. In a covalent bond, two atoms share electrons, and their wavefunctions overlap, creating a bond.

- **Molecular Orbital Theory:** This theory extends quantum mechanical principles to molecules, suggesting that atomic orbitals combine to form molecular orbitals. Electrons fill bonding and anti-bonding molecular orbitals, which affect the stability and properties of the molecule.

---

## 4. Hybridization:

---

## 5. Density Functional Theory (DFT):
One of the most widely used methods in quantum chemistry, DFT approximates the electron density of a molecule or solid to predict properties like the total energy, bond lengths, and reaction pathways. It's computationally less intensive than other quantum methods, making it practical for large systems.

---

## 6. Spectroscopy and Quantum Transitions:
Quantum chemistry plays a key role in understanding how atoms and molecules absorb or emit light (photons) when their electrons move between energy levels. This explains many types of spectroscopy, such as infrared (IR), ultraviolet-visible (UV-Vis), and nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) spectroscopy.
