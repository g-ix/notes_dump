# ORIGIN:
The word "biology" comes from two Greek words: bios (βίος), meaning "life," and logos (λόγος), meaning "study of". Therefore, biology literally means "the study of life". The term was introduced in the late 18th and early 19th centuries and replaced older terms like "natural history". 

---

### Greek origins: 
The word is a compound of the Greek roots bios and logos. 

---

### Meaning:
- Bios (βίος) translates to "life". 
- Logos (λόγος) translates to "study of," "knowledge," or "science". 

---

### Modern usage: 
The term "biology" emerged in the modern sense around 1799, though it appeared in earlier texts in slightly different contexts. Before this, the study of life was often referred to as "natural history".
