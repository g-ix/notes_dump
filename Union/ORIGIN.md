#ORIGIN:
The word "union" originates from the Latin unio, meaning "oneness" or "unity," which comes from the Latin word unus, meaning "one". It entered the English language in the 14th century from Middle French and Late Latin, describing the act of joining things together. 

---

### Latin root:
The ultimate origin is the Latin unus ("one"), through the Late Latin word unio ("oneness, unity").
